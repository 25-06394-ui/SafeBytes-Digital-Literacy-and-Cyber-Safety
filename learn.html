Passwords & Authentication

Refers to how you secure your online accounts. It includes creating strong passwords, enabling two-factor authentication (2FA), and avoiding password sharing. Strong authentication keeps your accounts safe from hackers and unauthorized access.

Critical Thinking

It means examining whether information is real, reliable, and safe. It involves checking sources, questioning what you see, and not believing everything you read on the internet. This skill helps you avoid misinformation, scams, and harmful content.

Wrong Usage of Digital Technology

Spreading Disinformation/Fake News

Using the ability to create and share content quickly online to spread deliberately false information or manipulated media, rather than using critical thinking skills to verify sources before sharing.

Plagiarism and Copyright Infringemen

Copying and pasting large sections of text from websites for school assignments without proper citation, or illegally downloading and distributing copyrighted music, movies, or software.

Sharing Private Information Online

Inappropriately posting sensitive personal details, such as passwords, home addresses, or financial information, on social media or unverified websites is a typical mistake.
